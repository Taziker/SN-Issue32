SAFETY CHAMPION
 

Daniel Annandale 
 

Whilst carrying out third party works on North Bridge (Edinburgh), Daniel noticed there was an open edge left without any handrails over the North Pier. Believing someone could easily fall into the open area which is about a 7ft drop.

 

Daniel took action by raising the issue to his manager and erecting a protective handrail around the leading edge

 

The process of adding these handrails to similar work areas has been made mandatory to prevent a reoccurrence.

 
 

STANDARD UPDATE
 

NR/L2/OHS/00130 - 

This standard allows work to take place near the railway, without the need to apply the control measures within NR/L2/OHS/019 Safety of People working On or. Near the Line, but it must be done in a controlled environment.

The main focus of the update is within Module 1: â€˜Platform Within a Station Environment'.


It has now been clarified that when more than 1.25m from the platform edge, and a risk assessment supports this, activities such as taking photographs and visual surveys, such as a reasonable passenger might do, can be carried out without segregation under NR/L2/OHS/00130.

In the main body of the standard, which contains the requirements applicable to all 4 modules, the requirement for a COSS competent person to be present on site to oversee the actual works has been clarified. For example, it is now clear that once a fixed barrier is in place, the site controller does not need to be present during works, as long as they have provided the person accountable for overseeing the works with an emergency response plan.

 

Compliance with these updates is 02/09/2023

CLOSE CALLS
 

PASMA Tower - No toe boards

Operatives were found to be working at height on a PASMA tower with no toe boards in place. The works were stopped and toe boards were installed.

 

Toe boards are to be installed on towers as per the manufacturer's instructions.

Unauthorised Scaffold Alteration

During a weekly scaffold inspection it was found that an unauthorised modification had taken place on a scaffold where a bracing tube had been cut by a contractor needing to get access to carry out another task.

The scaffold was immediately returned to its original state, and briefing was carried out on site in relation to unauthorised modifications.

Scaffold should only be modified by a competent scaffolder, if the scaffold does not accommodate the works you are carrying out speak to you Site Manager who can arrange for a Scaffold Supervisor to attend site
 

GOOD PRACTICE
 

Gaps in Scaffold Boards

It was observed on a project that a scaffold contractor has used sections of PVC profile to cover gaps in between boards to prevent materials falling in between. 

 These sections of PVC are quick and easy to install without fixings, and can be reused.

Emergency Station in Wales

The emergency response items have been setup in the work area providing the team quick access to emergency equipment.
